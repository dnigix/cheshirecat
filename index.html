Hi! I'm broadly interested in:

  • Simplicity and beauty
  • Understanding fundamental systems and solving problems through hacking
  • Pathological visualisation
  • Algebraic topology, differential geometry, complex analysis, enumerative combinatorics and operators
  • Artificial and biological minds
  • Quantum field theory and high-energy physics
  • Solving quantum gravity with artificial scientists
  • Computational models of minds and cells
  • Modernist literature, especially The Gift and everything Nabokov wrote before Ada
  • David Foster Wallace's essays (especially on tennis and E Unibus Pluram)
  • Samurai Champloo and Vagabond
  • Bach's fugues
  • Decreasing pDoom
  • Shigaraki pottery
  • Rock gardens
  • Rinzai Zen

The main reason I built this website is to have an archive - with propely formatted LaTeX - of things I care about, and therefore try to think about. The hope is that if I write down every tiny sliver of genius that ever pops into my head, I'll eventually have ideas I feel proud of. David Lynch said that if you don't write down a good idea, you get a 'real yearning to blow your brains out'. David Lynch has had many more good ideas that I've ever had, so I think he's probably worth listening to. I tried using Instagram and Substack for this goal, but Substack doesn't have built-in LaTeX support (slop!), and Instagram is pretty stupid. I want to use my short time on Earth to learn as much as I can. If you think we'll get along or you want to talk to me, feel free to email me - my policy is to always respond to people who demonstrate genuine interest, no matter what.
